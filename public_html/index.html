Backpacking

To determine what you need to bring on a backpacking trip, you should consider how far you plan to hike, how remote the location is and what the weather forecast will be. Think about these categories when getting ready for a trip. Backpacking gear ,clothing/footwear, camp kitchen , navigation ,food and water health hygiene, emergency items , and lastly tools for repairs to gear. This list includes a couple of the bare minimums that are absolutely essential for a backing trip.

Essentials

  • Backpack
  • Backpacking tent
  • Sleeping bag
  • Headlamp/ flash light
  • Backpacking stove/kit and fuel
  • Water filtration kit :gravity filter or purifying tablets
  • Bear canister/food sack or hang bag
  • first aid kit
  • Emergency whistle

Hiking

If you are heading out for a day hike to explore nature with friends and family, or even by yourself. Both Santa Fe and Cibola national parks offer a broad selection of trails, as does national forest land. Think about these categories when getting ready for a trip. Hiking gear ,clothing/footwear, navigation ,food and water health hygiene, emergency items , and lastly personal items. This list include a couple essentials in order to have a fun hiking trip.

Essentials

  • Daypack
  • Boots or shoes suited to terrain
  • Water bottles and/or reservoir
  • First-aid kit or first-aid supplies
  • Sunscreen
  • ID
  • Compass
  • Moisture-wicking T-shirt
  • Headlamp or flashlight

Camping

Camping is also great way to get outside with family and friends. You can go camping in either Santa Fe or Cibola national parks, at private campgrounds, or in the backcountry . Part of the joy of camping is needing very little gear to have a good camping trip and be comfortable outdoors. Most national park campsites include a picnic table, a place to park your car and a spot to pitch your tent while backcountry campsites are less developed. This list include a couple essentials needed for a fun and comfortable camping trip.

Essentials

  • Tent and footprint, stakes
  • Sleeping bags
  • Camp chairs
  • Saw or axe for cutting firewood
  • Mallet or hammer for hammering tent stakes
  • Stove ,fuel and kitchen essentials I.e. knife plates napkins
  • food , water and Ice or ice substitutes
  • Dry bags, stuff sacks or clear plastic bins to store items
  • First-aid kit or first-aid supplies
